Research Statement

My research, broadly defined, seeks to understand the production and reproduction of social inequality and stratification patterns in contemporary societies, using China as a case and with an explicit comparative approach. As sociology shifts to mechanism- and processes-based research, my research agenda is two-fold. First, I investigate how different social groups are being incorporated or excluded in a variety of stratification processes and what mechanisms contribute to their respective outcomes. Secondly, given the current stratification patterns in the Chinese society, my research also aims to understand how different social groups perceive, respond and make sense of the mobility and inequality at hand.

My long term research agenda is to expand my current work on identifying the casual mechanisms that shape and strengthen the inequality trends and stratification patterns, by explicitly considering the sorting and selectivity processes through which different social groups end up in various social positions. My goal is to not only provide solid descriptions of the trend of social inequality and stratification patterns in contemporary China, but also to engage in dialogue with the general literature and build up theoretical frameworks that explain the production and reproduction of social stratification. I aim to utilize longitudinal and international data and situate the case of China in a comparative and historical context.
